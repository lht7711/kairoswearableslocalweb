T-BAND
Complete smartwatch technology in a watch band.
DESIGNED TO WORK WITH ANY ANALOG WRIST WATCH

Kairos T-band launched on November 2014 and immediately gained global media spotlight for its unique design concept. Instead of making another smartwatch, Kairos wanted to turn the watch band into a wearable device in order for the wearer to enjoy wearing his or her analog wrist watch while enjoying the benefits that a smartwatch can provide.
VERSION 3 (FINAL PRODUCTION SPEC) – Estimated Launch Date: Summer 2017
Over 2 years of R&D, over 15 design variations and making multiple prototypes, Kairos is now finally in the final stages of prepareing for mass production by Q1/Q2 of 2017, to be shipped to retailers and consumers by summer of 2017.
T-BAND DEVELOPMENT JOURNEY

CLICK THE CIRCLE TO SEE PHOTOS

ORIGINAL VERSION

The T-band idea came to light in 2013 – together with the Hybrid Smartwatch concept. After creating a POC with key suppliers, the project was launched on November 2014.

Dec 2014

VERSION 1.1

The first fully working prototypes were created by January 2015 and was displayed at Baselworld in March. Kairos didn’t want to simply put “fitness sensors and a battery” into the band. The aim was to have a full-scale smartwatch technology into the band. Unfortunately, this created a major problem with size. It forced Kairos to re-engineer the main PCB to an incredibly small size.

Jan 2015

VERSION 2

After months of engineering work, Kairos was able to come up with a smart watch PCB that was just 15.5mm x 18mm in size – smallest smartwatch PCB in the world. It was barely the size of an adult fingernail. Kairos was faced with another challenge – flexibility and ergonomics. Having electronics on the side profile of the wrist meant it had to be flexible, light, slim, and comfortable to wear. Kairos teamed up with one of the world’s largest watch component manufacturers and developed Version 2 of the T-band.

Nov 2015

VERSION 2.2

More prototypes were created and various tests were done. The design was “90% ready” and Kairos contemplated in going into production with this design. However, during a more lengthy testing period, Kairos found few design flaws which included waterproof issues, links not being stable and higher degree of discomfort for people with slim wrists. It was a difficult decision, but Kairos decided to go back to the drawing board and start again.

Mar 2016

VERSION 3 (FINAL)

By this time, more than 2 years have passed after creating the first POC. However, Kairos was more than confident that the knowledge and experience that it gained was extremely valuable and the long development time was well worth it. The new design eliminated all of the issues from previous models while making it much more reliable, mass-production capable, and less costly to manufacture. Kairos is now working on making several prototypes for final testing by Q1/Q2 of 2017 in order to go into production by summer of 2017.
