THE IMPORTANCE OF BLOOD PRESSURE
According to Medscape (WebMD), Hypertension (High blood pressure) is the #1 risk factor for premature death worldwide.”

The CDC also reports that 1 in 3 Americans (over 70,000,000) suffer from hyper tension.

The Heart & Stroke Foundation has claimed that day to day blood pressure measurement is the best way to monitor and mitigate risks from hypertension.

Blood Pressure data can reveal a lot of information about the person’s arteries health conditions as well as various cardiovascular status.

HOW DO YOU MEASURE BLOOD PRESSURE?

For decades, blood pressure was monitored by getting two sets of numbers: systolic and diastolic blood pressure – ie: 120/80 mmHg. Systolic BP (upper number) is the highest pressure when your heart rate beats and pushes the blood around your body. Diastolic (bottom number) is the lowest pressure when your heart relaxes between beats.

WHAT IS PULSE PRESSURE?

Kairos TactArray™ is able to gather and monitor pulse pressure. Pulse pressure is the difference between systolic and diastolic pressure readings. It is also measured in millimeters of mercury (mmHg). It represents the force that the heart generates each tie it contracts.

For example, if your resting blood pressure is 120/80 mmHg, your pulse pressure is 40. Pulse pressure is also an easier way to monitor your blood pressure levels.

POTENTIAL PULSE PRESSURE RANGES

If your pulse pressure (PP) is between greater than 60-70mmHg you have high blood pressure (hypertension). If it is between 30 to 50mmHg, your blood pressure is within the normal range. If it less than 20-30, your BP numbers are considered to be low.

For adults older than age 60, a pulse pressure greater than 60 can be a useful predictor of heart attacks. A higher pulse pressure (greater than 60) may reflect leaky heart valves as well.

HOW ACCURATE IS IT?

We are confident that TactArray™ will become the “gold standard” in blood pressure measurements in the years to come. Here are some of our reasons.

  • Measurement is retrieved from the radial artery on the wrist (4.7mm from the skin) which has a stronger and more accurate pulse than the brachial artery in the arm (20mm from the skin).
  • 0.01 PSI sensitivity – with the ultra-sensitive tactile capacitive sensor, TactArray™ is able to retrieve extremely accurate readings.
  • TactArray™ uses actual pressure sensors rather than optical IR or LED sensors that “guesstimate” BP numbers.

Lab tests proved TactArray™ to be extremely reliable and accurate – an average repeatability error of only 3 mmHg was achieved. In comparison, an American oscillometric blood pressure monitoring device was tested to have an average repeatability error of 15mmHg and as high as 60mmHg.
