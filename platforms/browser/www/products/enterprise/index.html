WEARABLE DEVICE DESIGNED FOR FIRE FIGHTERS
According to the U.S. Fire Administration, stress and overexertion – including prolonged exposure to heat, account for 50% or more of firefighter line of duty deaths.

Individual health status, fitness level, medication and hydration levels also have a big impact.

PERSONAL DATA

KNOWING individual health conditions PRIOR to an event coupled with REAL-TIME health vital data (including continuous body temp readings and heart rate readings) can be a LIFE-SAVING feature for fighters.

Fitness and health data are automatically fed into the database along with manually inputted data about the users age and body info (prior medical conditions, height, weight, etc.)

ACTIVE FIELD DATA

Kairos has designed a wearable device that is equipped with on board ECG (HR) sensor, 9 Axis sensors (Fitness), GPS, Continuous body temp monitoring sensor and CO (carbon monoxide) gas sensor into a small “bracelet type” wearable device for firefighters.

When the device is set to active mode, it will monitor and send out other information including body temperature and CO gas readings to the operator.

GPS can be set to send out location pings to the operators in order for them to track active and inactive volunteer fire fighters (optional feature that can be controlled – turned off by the user).

ACTION

Data analyzing algorithm will compare and monitor the readings in real time and assist the operators and fire chief to control the situation on an individual level.

CONNECTION

Standard connection is via Bluetooth (BLE) while an optional on-board 4G connection is in the works. 4G connection will avoid the need to have a companion smartphone on the body to send out location and other health data to operations.

“TactArray™ Pulse Pressure Sensor available as an option.”

Kairos Firefighter project is scheduled to launch by summer of 2017. Kairos is currently working with several government agencies across 4 countries to help customize this solution.

Workers exposed to hot indoor environments or hot and humid conditions outdoors are at risk of heat-related illness, especially those doing heavy work tasks or using bulky or non-breathable protective clothing and equipment. Some workers might be at greater risk than others if they have not built up a tolerance to hot conditions, or if they have certain health conditions.

Heat related illnesses include Heat Stroke, Heat Exhaustion, Heat Cramps and Heat Rash. In severe cases, an individual with these types of illness can faint, have fast heart beat, muscle spasms and seizures which can cause further damage to their health.

PROJECT HIGHLIGHT

This project was first proposed to Kairos by a multi-billion dollar corporation in South Korea called POSCO. POSCO has several subsidiaries including POSCO Steel and POSCO E&C. Heat-exhaustion and physical stress is one of the biggest factors that leads to accidents in factory settings. Some construction sites on the other hand requires welding which uses LPG gases. LPG gas emits harmful CO gases which is odorless. These safety-hazard situations have prompted POSCO to create a wearable device that can monitor health vitals in real time while also monitoring the surrounding environment.


Another important factor was to own 100% of the data collected by the wearable device.


Since Kairos has its own hardware and firmware, Kairos was well equipped to custom create this solution for POSCO.


First prototypes of this project will be ready by Spring 2017.
